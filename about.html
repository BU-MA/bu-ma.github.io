Events

brief description of what events we host, and noting that they can see past events + our events for the current semester at the events page.

  • Putnam Problem-solving sessions: Practice for the Putnam exam, and solving problems from past exams. Lead by Professor Jared Weinstein.
  • Talks: Both professor-led and student-led talks, on everything from math in music theory to the most abstract nonsense humanity has conceived of. If you're interested in giving a talk, contact us!
  • Integration bee: A fun end to the semester. Inspired by MIT's integration bee, but less competitive and more fun. Even if you don't want to participate, you can come to watch physicists fail at solving Gaussians.
  • Social events: more chill nights where we do things like puzzles or crafts, or host an ice cream social, or have inter-club events.
  • Advising: helping students figure out their course trajectory, sharing research experience, possibly even discussing grad school
  • Other: We're always coming up with ideas for new meetings. If there's an event you would like to see happen, we would LOVE to hear from you! You can find our contact information here.
